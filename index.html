Exporing Social Media Data

In an effort to understand the effects social media has on users, we have decided to explore social media and how marketing tactics affect spending on popular social media sites as well as it’s over all reception by users. This is important as it better informs users of strategies used by sites to lure users into spending as well as allowing for the marketers themselves too see how effectively they are marketing on certain platforms to different demographics of consumers. In this project we have successfully found data on data.world. The datasets we chose from data.world was collected via survey on an app known as “Whatsgoodly”.

Data Summary

The first dataset we used included 6 different columns that addressed different pieces of information about each individual surveyed. There were a total of 2676 of people who responded to the survey. The survey asked the following question: What social platform has influenced your online shopping most? Respondents had the following answers: Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, Twitter, None. The survey also categorized users into different demographics such as Mobile, Web, Gender, University, Custom, and other groupings allowing us to extrapolate different trends based on these demographics.

The second dataset we have analyzed has 6 different columns with a total of 10427 survey participants. This survey inquired the following; What do you think when an influencer is obviously selling a product?, What social platform has influenced your online shopping most?, What is the most important trait when defining an influencer?, Who are your favorite people to follow online?, Does it annoy you when popular social media accounts endorse products or services?, What do you consider an influencer? The individuals surveyed responded to these inquiries along with demographic information exactly the same as the dataset above such as Mobile, Web, Gender, University, Custom.

Opinions on Social Media Marketing

As mentioned above, one of the questions asked in the Whatsgoodly surveys was “What do you think when an influencer is obviously selling a product?”. We categorized the answers so that their responses were categorized as positive, negative, or neutral. This allowed us to make observations on how different demographics felt about social media marketing.

We started by looking at the overall opinion of social media marketing. Below is a pie chart that shows this distribution according to the surveys.

We thought that a pie chart was an effective way of visualizing the distribution because we found that there was a distinct enough difference in count between the three groups. This was assisted by the easily readable percentages within the sections. As seen in the chart, most people that took the survey had a negative or neutral opinion on social media marketing, with almost half of the survey takers having a negative reaction to ads.

We took a deeper dive into the question and looked at how different economic classes felt about social media marketing.

We thought that a stacked bar chart was an effective way of visualizing the opinion counts because there was an easy way to show the amount of people in each category. Although there were significantly differing amounts of respondents in each category, the percentages in each economic class stay roughly the same. They are also very similar to the overal percentages shown in the previous pie chart.

Effectiveness

To effectively understand how social media marketing affects user interaction, we knew we had to figure out which platforms had the most effective ads. The pie chart below shows the distribution of answers to the Whatsgoodly survey question on which platform has influenced their online shopping.

Since there was only one variable to account for, we thought that a pie chart was effective at showing the percentages. We chose to keep the “None” category in the bar chart because this section represents the users that are not influenced by social media marketing. 40% of respondents responded with “None”, indicating that there was a significant amount of people who weren’t affected by the ads. Of those who were influenced by social media ads, Instagram users were the most affected with 31% and Facebook in second with 20.4%. Note that Instagram is owned by Facebook, which might be the reason why they account for the greatest influence in this survey.
